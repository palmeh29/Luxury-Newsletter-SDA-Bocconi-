Cover Story · Craftsmanship

LVMH Reopens Its Ateliers to the World

For the first time since 2022, LVMH will throw open the doors of 46 of its houses across 65 sites in 11 countries for the return of Journées Particulières - the group's celebrated open-house weekend. Visitors will step inside Celine's ready-to-wear workshops on Rue Vivienne and Louis Vuitton's family home and atelier in Asnières, just outside Paris.

This sixth edition takes a deliberate "less is more" approach, says Antoine Arnault, with fewer maisons and a sharper focus on the artisans themselves. A singular multi-house event in Shanghai will bring French and Italian savoir-faire closer to Chinese audiences- a timely gesture as the industry pivots from fashion cycles back toward heritage, craft and the true value of an object.

This Month

Companies · Acquisition

Chanel Acquires Charvet

Chanel has taken ownership of Charvet, the storied Parisian shirtmaker, vowing to preserve its independence and rare savoir-faire. The move follows Matthieu Blazy's sold-out €3,900 debut shirt and revives whispers of a menswear future the house is careful to downplay.

Retail · Consolidation

Saks Finalises Neiman Marcus Deal

The $2.7 billion merger of America's storied luxury department stores closes at last, with Amazon and G-III Apparel Group among the backers. The combined Saks Global reshapes the top of the US retail landscape amid a cooler spending climate.

Watches & Jewellery · Payments

Luxury Weighs Crypto at the Counter

As Bitcoin scales new highs, high-end brands and retailers are reconsidering crypto payments and watch specialists are eyeing a crypto-fuelled comeback, hopeful that a new wave of digital wealth revives appetite for fine mechanical timepieces.

Executive Memo · The lvmh Market

Luxury Has a Pricing Problem. What Now?

A decade of steep price hikes has strained the relationship between big luxury houses and their aspirational customers. With the boom over and shoppers questioning value, the industry faces a delicate recalibration- how to protect the aura of exclusivity without alienating the very clients who fuelled its rise.
